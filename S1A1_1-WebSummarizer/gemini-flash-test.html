Neural Network Architectures and Transformers

Modern AI systems rely heavily on transformer architectures, which have revolutionized how machines process and understand sequential data. These models use attention mechanisms to dynamically focus on relevant parts of the input, enabling them to capture long-range dependencies and complex relationships that were previously impossible to model effectively.

The transformer architecture consists of multiple layers of self-attention and feed-forward neural networks, each contributing to the model's ability to understand context and generate coherent, contextually appropriate responses. This design has proven particularly effective for natural language processing tasks, including summarization, translation, and question answering.
